## Using Text-Based Elements
### Headings
Headings are block-level elements that come in six different rankings, `<h1>` through `<h6>`. Headings help to quickly break up content and establish hierarchy. They also serve as key identifiers for users reading a page.

Each heading level should be used where it is semantically valued, not to make text bold or big.

```html
<h1>Heading Level 1</h1>
<h2>Heading Level 2</h2>
<h3>Heading Level 3</h3>
<h4>Heading Level 4</h4>
<h5>Heading Level 5</h5>
<h6>Heading Level 6</h6>
```

### Paragraphs
Headings are often followed by supporting paragraphs, which are identified using the `<p>` block-level element.

```html
<p>I am the opening paragraph!</p>

<p>I am the closing paragraphs!</p>
```

### Bold Text with Strong
The `<strong>` inline-level element makes text bold, placing an importance on the text. `<b>` is the other element that bolds text.

Semantically, `<strong>` gives strong importance to text, and thus is the most popular option for bolding text. `<b>`, on the other hand, semantically means to stylistically offset text.

```html
<!-- Strong Importance -->
<p><strong>Caution:</strong> Boiling hot!<p>

<!-- Stylistically Offset -->
<p>This recipe calls for <b>bacon</b> and <b>baconnaise</b>.</p>
```

### Italicize Text with Emphasis
The `<em>` inline-level element italicizes text, placing emphasis on it. `<i>` is the other element that italicizes text.

Semantically, `<em>` places a stressed emphasis on text, and thus is the most popular option for italicizing text. `<i>`, on the other hand, semantically conveys text in an alternative voice or tone, almost as if it were placed in quotation marks.

```html
<!-- Stressed Emphasis -->
<p>I <em>love</em> Tokyo!</p>

<!-- Alternative Voice or Tone -->
<p>The name <i>Ryu</i> means a dragon.</p>
```
